i was thinking; why is it so hard to make friends nowadays? so i was looking back to how i first made friends
<br><br>
the thing is, the friends i made in nursery, are still my friends. i would do anything for them. but they're only my friends because of proximity. we only ever became friends because we saw each other for like 14 years lmfao.
<br><br>
these are friends who i would accept in any condition. like, there isn't anything they can do, to make me not like them. and that's because there wasn't a specific thing about them that made me like them in the first place. proximity friendships eliminate considering characters and personalities. especially when you're growing up and don't know what's right or wrong.
<br><br>
i have moral boundaries, and i wouldn't want them to cross those. like if i found out they're cheating on their partner, im blowing their jig up, i'm not covering that shit up for them. i'll stand by their side and help them be better, but i won't help them be bad. they can continue trying to be bad, but i'll continue trying to help them be better. but the thing is, this only applies to those 4 idiots, and no one else.
<br><br>
for everyone else, i have strict boundaries that when crossed, i'd rather burn the bridge than have my boundary broken. and that's because, all my other friendships are not proximity friendships.
<br><br>
everyone else, i'm friends with, because i like something about them. there's always a reason to befriend them. this might sound un-noble to some but, it is what it is.
